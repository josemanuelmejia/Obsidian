
At my earlier recognition of social movements theory I was already embedded in a field of political contention highly developed. The Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos is not only the elder university of the whole continent but also a living block of historical text clashing in class struggle. While the political activism outside the traditional social movement organizations was constrained by the authoritarian fraction of the bureaucratic bourgeoisie identified with the mafia leaded by the Fujimori clan and the CIA agent Vladimiro Montesinos that remain in the Judiciary and the Armed Forces, gradually centralizing the class interests of the oligarchy, commercial, industrial and banking domestic bourgeoisies fractions of the Peruvian bureaucratic bourgeoisie during the post-transition of the 2000s.

The tipping point in this process was the successful coup d'état executed by the militaries loyal to the narco-bourgeoisie empowered by its several attempts to impeach the popular elected president Pedro Castillo, channeled by the National Congress, the National Constitutional Court, the Judiciary, and the complete Armed Forces. The political struggle against the reestablishment of the dictatorship left more than 70 peasants, workers and students killed by the army and the police in the Peruvian episode of the authoritarian reversion in Latin America, contested broadly and bravely by the massive stroke of an antagonist political consciousness emergent from peoples blood.
