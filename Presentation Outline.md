Devil lives in the details.

Marx is tracing the determinants of the exchange process particular of the bourgeois society in its initial conditions. The process of exchange is the starting point in the circulation of capital under its commodity form, beginning not with the production of the commodity as use-values, its by the realization of them as exchange-value what allows its consumption as use-values. In the transition between the exchange of use-values, and the exchange of commodities, there is a determinant detail. And more than a detail, the particularity of the exchange process, an original mistake, a social accident, acted then thought: treat something that is not for being appropriated as a commodity. 
In the exchange process the capitalist as bearer of a commodity, : treating something that is not a use-value as an exchange-value, and fix its use as a mean for exchange while appearing in the process of exchange as if it would have the exchange-value form of commodities. The determinants of the exchange process are rooted in the history of the formal social movement of the commodities, because:

- the exchange of use-values in upward scale-shift, requires a relative form of value reached by social accident by one commodity which is both commodity and money: gold, and also other precious metals in history, that is fixed as an universal equivalent..
- the transportation of the use-values in an exchange without a relative form of value, have N universal equivalents and the volume of the exchanged use-values will be double for each transaction for both of the bearers.
- Gold mining was already a branch of production centralized in the hand of capitalist production, before, during, and after the transition of mercantile capital into industrial and finance capital. So the social accident of the expanded circulation of gold-money as capital and mainly the decision to use it as mean for exchange, as universal equivalent, is not an accident for the individual gold bearer who as bearer of any other commodity want to exchange every other commodity without alienating his money-gold in the process of exchange as an exchange-value. Using pure money, as the representation of the exchange value of its money-gold. 

The historical expansion and intension of the process of exchange is the process of taking everything that is possible as *something* (LCWV38P108L17) to appropriate, as if it was a commodity, with the purpose of appear as its owner as an exchange-value, seeking to receive a equivalent exchange-value in form of the most circulated exchange-value as mean of payment. And therefore, by the escalation of its compulsive subsumption over circulation as determinant of the: "need to give an external expression to this opposition [between use-value and value which is latent in the nature of the commodity] *for the purposes of commercial intercourse* produces the drive towards an independent form of value, which finds neither rest nor peace until an independent form [] has been achieved by the differentiation of commodities into commodities and money. 


The first distinction treated is the one between the commodity and the bearer. 
The comparison between the commodity and its bearer. For the [bearer] his commodity possesses no direct use-value.[[KV1C2P179L13]]
For himself its only direct use-value is as a bearer of exchange-value, and consequently, a means of exchange. [[KV1C2P179L15]]

The second is the determination of a universal equivalent as a consequence of the expansion of the exchange process.

From the perspective of the individual each of the bearers treat his own commodity in exchange as the universal equivalent for all other commodities.

The third one corresponds to the transformation of the money-commodity to monetarized capital.

> "In their difficulties our commodity-owners think like Faust: 'In  the beginning was the deed.' They have therefore already acted before thinking. The *natural laws of the commodity* have manifested themselves in the natural instinct of the owners of commodities. They can only bring their commodities into relation as  values, and therefore as commodities, by bringing them into an  opposing relation with some one other commodity, which serves  as the universal equivalent. We have already reached that result by  our analysis of the commodity. But only the action of society can  turn a particular commodity into the universal equivalent. The  social action of all other commodities, therefore, sets apart the  particular commodity in which they all represent their values. The natural-form of this commodity thereby becomes the socially recognized equivalent form. Through the agency of the social  process it becomes the specific social function of the commodity which has been set apart to be the universal equivalent. It thus  becomes money." [[KV1C2P180L24]]
