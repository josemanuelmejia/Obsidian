Provide a brief outline of your research project (approx. 300 word): The antimining movement is currently at the front of the struggle for environmental justice in Los Andes and in Latin America. The militarism with which the expansion of the mining extractive frontier advancement is evidenced in the assassination of indigenous and cholo peasants by paramilitary squads linked to the domestic narco-bourgeoisies, and by the army and police militarized repression. These assassinations of peasants occurred during the execution of campaigns of struggle against the dispossession and destruction of land and water by mining production, when the organized indigenous and cholo peasantry contests the bullets and war grenades of state armies with self-crafted and improvised shields, slingshots, and their heads, eyes, legs, arms and chests. In the afterwards of the antimining campaigns of struggle the criminalization of peasants leaders, environmental activists, young and old indigenous and cholo woman and man judicialized by the mining corporations accused of eco-”terrorism” for defending the lagoons, paramos, mountains, and the total biodiversity -reduced to the ontological objects conceptualized as land and water-, as vital sources for the social reproduction not only of the peasantry, but for the rural proletariat of the nearest towns and cities, and for the future of human and non-human life. The project proposed will develop fieldwork during the development of the antimining campaigns at Ecuador and Perú, from a research-activist approach. The collaboration with the indigenous-cholo peasantry organizations which constitute the antimining movement in Los Andes, will produce artistic, multimedia, and scholar contents oriented to impact on the conditions in which the anti-capitalist and environmental justice indigenous-cholo peasantry struggles against mining dispossession and destruction of water and land in Los Andes and Latin America. 2. Describe your own research interests and how they align with one of the Orfalea Center’s Thematic Research Clusters and research themes (approx. 300 word): My research-activist interests are focused on the antisystemic effects of the antimining struggles. I’m approaching the militarism with which the antimining movements are being repressed as a feature of the breakout of the U.S. interstate hegemony, in which the securitization foreign policy over Los Andes is being executed by militarization since 2019 when the authoritarian neoliberal reversion process of regimes de-democratization started in Latin America after almost 20 years of neoliberal-democratization romance, at the intercourse of the Ukraine-Russia and the Palestine-Israel-U.S. imperialists wars of repartition. For the analysis of the historical juncture in which the antimining movement in Los Andes is embedded, I’m building a bridge from the historical political sociology to a Marxist critique of the political economy of imperialism. The connection between the antimining struggles and radical environmental justice social movement’s claims is explicit as the indigenous-cholo peasantry is stopping with their chests and improvised shields the destruction of land, water and biodiversity. Contesting effectively but in complete asymmetry of political resources the expansion of the extractive frontier, which is advancing through the Andes toward the Amazonian forest, considered as the Lung of Humanity. 3. Please describe in a few sentences how your global partner is crucial to completing your research (approx. 200 word): The peasantry organizations in Los Andes which are assuming the environmental justice struggles against imperialists mining dispossession and destruction, have different trajectories of organizations and institutionalization. Considering the multilevel polity approach to the antisystemic effects of antimining campaigns as anticapitalists and radical environmental justice struggles, three different organizations from three different organizational scales will participate as global partners on the development of the production of the itemized items proposed in section 3. The Confederation of Indigenous Nationalities of Ecuador (CONAIE), the Antimining National Front of Ecuador (FNA), and the United National Central of Nightwatchers Peasants (CUNARC) organizations (Rondas Campesinas) of Peru. The conditions of asymmetry in political resources for contesting the state violence as a feature of militarism during the expansion of the extractive frontier in Los Andes, are driving the antimining struggles to develop further the comprehensiveness and intelligibility of the indigenous-cholo historical resistance not only as a denounce for the recognition of the concreteness of its historicity, but also as a legitimate maneuver to anticipate the escalation of militarism over Los Andes. Under this precise juncture the antimining movement embodied in the CONAIE, FNA, and CUNARC develops a process of internationalization, seeking for allies for environmental justice broader campaigns as a preventive measure against the escalation of militarism by the creation of imperialists wars (as Ukraine and Palestine) and neo-securitization military interventions motivated by the expansion of the mining extractive frontier as a competence for guaranteeing the sources of raw materials as minerals vital for the capital reproduction circuit of the uppers strata of the international bank state capitalists trusts finance capital. 5.
