The peasantry organizations in Los Andes which are assuming the environmental justice struggles against imperialists mining dispossession and destruction, have different trajectories of organizations and institutionalization. Considering the multilevel polity approach to the antisystemic effects of antimining campaigns as anticapitalists and radical environmental justice struggles, three different organizations from three different organizational scales will participate as global partners on the development of the production of the itemized items proposed in section 3.

The Confederation of Indigenous Nationalities of Ecuador (CONAIE), the Antimining National Front of Ecuador (FNA), and the United National Central of Nightwatchers Peasants (CUNARC) organizations (Rondas Campesinas) of Peru.

The conditions of asymmetry in political resources for contesting the state violence as a feature of militarism during the expansion of the extractive frontier in Los Andes, are driving the antimining struggles to develop further the comprehensiveness and intelligibility of the indigenous-cholo historical resistance not only as a denounce for the recognition of the concreteness of its historicity, but also as a legitimate maneuver to anticipate the escalation of militarism over Los Andes. Under this precise juncture the antimining movement embodied in the CONAIE, FNA, and CUNARC develops a process of internationalization, seeking for allies for environmental justice broader campaigns as a preventive measure against the escalation of militarism by the creation of imperialists wars (as Ukraine and Palestine) and neo-securitization military interventions motivated by the expansion of the mining extractive frontier as a competence for guaranteeing the sources of raw materials as minerals vital for the capital reproduction circuit of the uppers strata of the international bank state capitalists trusts finance capital.
