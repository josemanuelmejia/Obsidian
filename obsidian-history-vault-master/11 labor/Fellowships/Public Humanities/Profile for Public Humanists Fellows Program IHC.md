Short bio:

José Manuel is a M.A./Ph.D. student in the Sociology Department. His ongoing research on anti-mining class struggles developed by the Andean indigenous-cholo peasantry examines its antisystemic effects over capital reproduction.

Long bio:

My research in antisystemic movements and political contention have being formed within contexts connoted by episodes of contention as the October insurrection of 2019 in Quito (Ecuador). I ground my research on anti-mining struggles in Los Andes, where the indigenous-cholo peasantry organizations survived and developed subsumed under the coloniality of power, capital, and knowledge, but not in a passive manner as the history of indigenous, anticolonial and anti-imperialist resistances show widely. In this sense, my formation as a Marxist researcher in historical political sociology is aligned with the developing concrete needs of the Andean and Latin American anti-mining movements.

By a public humanist approach to anti-mining movements research, my participation in the IHC Public Humanities Graduate Fellows Program pursues the building of intelligible and useful theory for a broader public than the audience of specialized-scholars already empowered by their knowledge. In contrast, I work to develop collaborative and multidisciplinary research projects in partnership with anti-mining social movements organizations, public scholars, NGOs, and fundraising organizations, oriented to the empowerment of the indigenous-cholo struggles against mining dispossession and destruction of land and water.
