Public Humanities at once embrace and endorse knowledge itself as a public good. While some citizens may have a dim awareness that the medications they take, the technology in their kitchen, or the dramatic writing of their favorite film all derive from the kind of research and training that take place in universities, few who are not themselves students regard the university as an institution cen tral to their lives, and far fewer experience the university as an open invitation to intellectual engagement.
