Discussion of papers 

1. Background (Alycia)

- Families play an important part in the social reproduction of educational attainment
    
- Previous literature discusses parental characteristics and family attributes (e.g. SES, size, intact vs. non-intact family type) as central factors in educational trajectories
    
- Increasing literature on grandparents’ impact on their grandchildren’s education attainment
    
- In developing countries where grandparental educational attainment is fairly low, co-residence with grandparents and family structure may wield greater influence on grandchildren’s educational attainment
    

- This is especially so in a Mexican context in which grandparents’ co-residence is strongly associated with family resources and grandparents’ health status
    
- Co-residing with grandparents can have varying impacts such as resource competition (between children and adults in the household), monetary and emotional support, and/or assistance in caretaking
    
- Family structure situations (e.g. parental separation/divorce, family death) are a big factor in how beneficial economic and emotional support from grandparents is on the grandchild, thus resulting in varying influences on educational attainment
    

- This study engages in a comprehensive analysis of multiple family structures which is missing from previous literature: children raised in households where both parents are absent; where the father has died; and where the father is absent due to migration, separation/divorce, or other reasons - all of which have been identified as negatively impacting children’s grade completion
    
- This study contributes to educational stratification literature in Latin America in 3 ways:
    

- 1. Analyzes the impact of grandparents’ education on grandchildren’s school continuation (previous literature focused on parents only)
    
- 2. Extends analysis of grandparent/grandchild co-residence impact by examining the differential effect of grandparent’s co-residence as a function of grandparent’s educational attainment
    
- 3. Provides updated estimations of the influence of family background on school continuation in Mexico
    

- Theoretical Approach:
    

- Theoretical model based on a “life course perspective approach” to examine how family background transmits differential advantages to subsequent generations
    
- Educational attainment is analyzed as a process of sequences that children may or may not take:
    

- First transition: primary to secondary school
    
- Second transition: secondary to high school
    

- As such these transitions act as markers for the impact of grandparental co-residence, family structure, and grandparental education on educational attainment
    

2. Research Questions and Hypotheses  (Alycia)

- Research Questions:
    

1. Does grandparental’ co-residence influence grandchildren’s educational transitions, and does this differ based on family structure?
    
2. Does grandparental education influence children’s educational transitions, and does this differ by parental education level or co-residence with grandchildren?
    
3. Is the influence of grandparental education and co-residence more important in the first transition than the second?
    

• Hypotheses: How is the dependent variable related with your independent variables?
