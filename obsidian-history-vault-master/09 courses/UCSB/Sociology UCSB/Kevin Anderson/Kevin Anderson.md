- Curious about the use of the translation of Capital Book 1 by Ben Fawkes. I reviewed the English edition with which I'm used to work and also other English edition (I will cite conveniently) and there is an omission I weight as important in the first paragraph of the famous first section of the first chapter of the first book of Marx's Capital which is:

"The wealth of societies in which the capitalist mode of production prevails appears as an inmense collection of commodities; the individual commodity appears as its elementary form. Our investigation therefore begins with the analysis of the commodity" (Ben Fowkes translation).

"The wealth of those societies in which the capitalist mode of production prevails, presents itself as an inmense accumulation of commodities, its unite being a single commodity (Engels translation)".

In german:

Der Reichtum der Gesellshaften, in welchen kapitalistiche Produktionsweise herrscht, erscheint als eine ,,ungeheure Waren-sammlung", die einzelne Ware als seine Elementarform (seine Ausgangsform). Unsere Untersuchung beginnt daber mit der Analyse (der Zergliederung) der Ware.

The wealth of societies, in which the capitalist mode of production prevails, appears as an "immense collection of commodities", the individual commodity as its elementary form (its initial form). However, our investigation begins with the analysis (dismemberment) of the goods.
