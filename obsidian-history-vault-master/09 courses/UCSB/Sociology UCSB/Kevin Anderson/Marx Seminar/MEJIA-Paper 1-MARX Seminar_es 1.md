Giving special attention to the value metamorphosis to gold as the universal equivalent commodity, the money-commodity. It is also merely another effort to develop Marx’s critique of the political economy of capitalism, particularly his theory on the so-called original accumulation as the historical foundation of capital reproduction. Grasping particularly into the metamorphosizes of commodities into money and of money into capital to understand the formation of the real capital form of value. It is hoarded gold historically concentrating an aliquot part of all the monetary-form of value realized into the mass of accumulated gold, as capital presented in its real value form simultaneously self-reproducing, multiplying, and constantly self-equivalent. The subsumption of the exchange process to the circuit of capital reproduction Marx traced the determinants of the circulation process particularly for bourgeois society in its initial conditions. Conclusions The development of capital as a form of value developed necessarily after gold was already money, and money circulation had already subsumed the exchange process. Marx studied the initial conditions of the circulation process and the historical development of capital reproduction process as a form of value. The historical juncture among the process of commodities exchange and money circulation is denoted as original accumulation, because it connotes the origine of capital as capital, as the alien organism subsuming nature forces into its self-reproductive metabolism. Only after the formation of “America” resultant from the colonization process by the methods of primitive accumulation, dispossession, and pure militarism; there is a world-economy; the subsumption process of capital operated since its initial conditions at the world-economy scale. The reproduction metabolism development of the capital form of value is dependent on, or constrained by, the scale of the exchange process. It first relies on the production of a surplus product which becomes commodity-capital (C-M-C’), and, only after reaching that condition, then it functions as productive capital (measured as the total cost price) in the circuit of reproduction (M-C-M’). While capital is not always productive nor finance, as can be hoarded, it is real capital only if produces profits. Money, also, as capital, is organic in relation to capital, only if can bring real capital from the circuit of money circulation is functional for capital reproduction metabolism. Not all the money raised produced as a form of profit employing certain capital, can became capital, only the money which circulates as finance-productive capital, and the gold hoarded as real capital, are money-capital really. Overaccumulated money is not capital, until it becomes money that passes through the process of capital production. Furthermore, gold’s significance as the elementary form of the measure unit of price increased incrementally since the formation of the world-economy, not only in its ideal measure of value, but in its physical value form, accumulating constantly in the hoarded mass of commodity-money, the surplus-value historically produced by the exploitation of nature, and living human labour. As the commodity price of gold rises at every instance of the circulation process, accumulating in its physical form, a fixed aliquot part of everything consumed by every exchange mediated by money.
