	⁃	In this chapter Weber is developing his theory of rationality. In studying religion and religious sociology, Weber seeks to discover the epistemology (that is philosophical study of a branch of knowlege) and the ontology, or the very nature of of rationality and where it is headed. In constructing “rational types” which is an “elaboration of the interally most ‘consisten’ forms of practical behavior derived from fixed, given presuposition”, he uses rational types to compare to reality. In other words rational types are “the most rational forms which reality can assume” that “seeks to establish how far certain theoretically presentable, rational consequeneces have followed in reality and why they were not followed”. In my interpretation of Weber, theory thus functions to explain reality is therefore approximate and must be tested for how well it explains reality (p216).
