**Gold crashes to 10**\-**month low on selling spree**

261 words

5 April 2013

AIWFIE

English

Copyright 2013 Indian Express Online Media Pvt. Ltd.

Gold prices dropped to a 10-month low on Thursday as they fell by R100 to R29,500 per 10 grams due to sustained selling by stockists, influenced by a weak global trend.

However, silver recovered by R500 to R51,750 per kg on buying at low levels by industrial units. The metal had tumbled R2,250 in the last two sessions to hit a 15-month low on Wednesday.

Sentiment in gold remained bearish after it extended losses in global markets on the concern that investors are seeking higher returns in other assets as the global economy recovers.

Gold in global markets, which normally sets the price trend on the domestic front, lost 1% to $1,541.82 an ounce, the lowest level since May 30. Silver lost 1% to $26.73 an ounce, the lowest level since July 24 in Singapore.

In addition, sustained selling by stockists due to sluggish demand on account of the wedding off-season further fuelled the downtrend.

On the domestic front, gold of 99.95 and 99.5% purity fell further by R100 each to R29,500 and R29,300 per 10 grams, respectively. Sovereign held steady at R25,100 per piece of eight gram in limited deals. On the other hand, silver ready found low-level buying support from industrial units and recovered by R500 to R51,750 per kg.

Silver coins continued to be asked around the previous level of R78,000 for buying and R79,000 for selling of 100 pieces.
