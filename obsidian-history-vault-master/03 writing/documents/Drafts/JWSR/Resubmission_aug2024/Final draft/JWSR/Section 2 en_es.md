The antimining struggles have antisystemic effects obstructing the circuit of mining reproduction of certain finance capitals. By obstructive performances of struggle, the indigenous-cholo peasantry blockade the production and transportation of a compromised volume of mining commodity-capital, during an expected circulation time and with a predetermined organic composition. The increment of the time of circulation in the transformation of surplus-value into profit, delays the consumption of the finance capital invested in constant capital, increasing the difference between the rate of surplus-value and the rate of profit, an indeed transforming a reduced magnitude of surplus-value into total profit.

Indeed, the circuit of capital accumulation of the imperialist bourgeoisies cannot achieve the conversion of their finance capital into increased money-capital by profit. Because of the operation of the following mechanisms: i) the delay in the circulation time of the transformation of surplus-value into total profit, ii) disrupt the expected magnitudes of finance capitals organic composition advanced in the production of surplus-value and in its posterior transformation into total profit, which iii) trigger the gold hoarding by imperialists bank state trusts, and iv) challenges its militarist competition by means of imperialist wars or imperialists interventions for determining the relations of production at the "national" economies in which the mining precious "raw" materials are situated.

In this sense, the indigenous-cholo peasantry struggles challenge the circulation of the productive capital of the imperialists mining bourgeoisies by obstructing the production and circulation of mining commodities. The circuit of capital accumulation of the imperialist bourgeoisies cannot achieve the conversion of their productive capital into increased money-capital, that is, the transformation of surplus-value into profit. If mineral raw materials are not sold on the international market, the financial capital invested in mining cannot yield profits.
