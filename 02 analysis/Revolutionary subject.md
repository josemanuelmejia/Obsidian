Q4: How do anti-mining struggles in the Andes evidence the resurgence of the indigenous peasantry as a revolutionary subject at the present crisis of US hegemony and systemic chaos of global capitalism?

Argument: the indigenous category don't elude most of the violent facts of history that is colonization. This method of militarized capital accumulation was in the core of the interstate competition for mobile capital during the sixteenth century. America is the result of the primitive accumulation made by colonization as a "method" of capital accumulation, the colonization of the old worlds of the region where "America"  a planned and funded investment of the European financial capitalist class. The money-capital for the invasion and colonization of "America" was deposited in the interstate credit system, as a large-profits enterprise, colonial mining provide to the European capitalist class with the expected huge returns that motivates capital accumulation. The material expansion of capitalism as a world-system since then realized four systemic cycles of accumulation holding their financial expansion phases in Europe, and during all the XX century in the U.S. Around ten millions of indigenous peoples survive the destruction of their old civilization, the world didn't fit anymore in their consciousness. But in their consciousness survive a myth or a myth make sense in their consciousness, is the myth of the new indigenous, of the Yawar-Inti and the resurgence of the indigenous world by a revolutionary vendetta.

As the new indigenous and cholo intellectuals create the prophets of this myth, indigenous socialism forges at the beginnings of the XX century as the convergence of the "cholo group" with the indigenous peasantry already organizing themselves for indigenous revolution. Considering the position of the Andean indigenous not only in the domestic "gamonalist" regime of accumulation, but also in the global dynamic of capital accumulation, the history of indigenous class struggle definitely precedes socialism but most importantly instrumentalice it.

The labor of Mariátegui, Mayer and Valcárcel in the organization and creation of indigenous socialism, founds a indigenous reemergence myth already in operation. The revolution realizes as a vendetta for the new indigenous, their remanentes, their sons and grandsons. Revolution as a movement was already in operation in the Andes when the Amautas headed by José Carlos Mariátegui initiate their labor. As he writes in the prologue of Tempestad en los Andes de Valcárcel:

"Recuerdo el imprevisto e impresionante tipo de agitador que encontré hace cuatro años en el indio puneño Ezequiel Urviola. Este encuentro fue la más fuerte sorpresa que me reservó el Perú a mi regreso de Europa. Urviola representaba la primera chispa de un incendio por venir. Era el indio revolucionario, el indio socialista. Tuberculoso, jorobado, sucumbió al cabo da dos años de trabajo infatigable. Hoy no importa ya que Urviola no exista. Basta que haya existido. Como dice Valcárcel, hoy la sierra está preñada de espartacos" (Mariátegui en el prólogo a Valcárcel 1927, 11).

The "cholo group", as Quijano would state, is a vanguard of the indigenous resurgence made by the new indigenous, the revolutionary subject of which Valcárcel and Mariátegui would state were a part of.
