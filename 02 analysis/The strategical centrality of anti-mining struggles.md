Four arguments are provided for the strategical centrality of anti-mining struggles as an antisystemic movement in the current crisis of global capitalism:

1) Revolutionary subject: from the indigenous-peasant-miner to the indigenous-peasant-unique-front
2) Challenge imperialist domination
3) Antisystemic effects on capital accumulation extended reproduction mechanism
4) Class struggle critical juncture: the current systemic crisis of global capitalism, hegemony crisis and breakdown, and the natural limits of capitalism reproduction
