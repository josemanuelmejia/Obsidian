In writing down a model that will “explain y in terms of x ,” we must confront three issues. And third, how can we be sure we are capturing a ceteris paribus relationship between y and x (if that is a desired goal)? A better assumption involves the expected value of u given x . Because u and x are random variables, we can deﬁne the conditional distribution of u given any value of x .
