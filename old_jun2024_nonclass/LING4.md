This is a short presentation video for the LING4 coursework. My name is José Manuel Mejía and I'm a Marxist political sociologist from the Andes of South America. My main research topics are historical political sociology, contentious politics, antimining struggles, Marxist political economy, and indigenous socialism.
Currently, my research work is dedicated to trace two mechanisms by which antimining struggles had antisystemic effects over the modern capitalist world-system experiencing an ecological, overaccumulation, and global hegemonic crisis: 1) by obstructing the mining capital reproduction circuit, and 2) by challenging the shared imperialist domination guaranteeing the militarized repression of antimining struggles.
My main case studies are the antimining campaigns of struggle headed by the Antimining National Front of Ecuador against the initiation of Chinese and Canadian transnational mining projects, and the emblematic antimining movement in the northern and southern Peruvian Andes of Peru against US gold and Chinese copper mining projects already in production.
Among the main methods of research I employ in my work are: ethnography of violent extractive contention, archival research, protest event analysis, qualitative comparative analysis and process tracing.
