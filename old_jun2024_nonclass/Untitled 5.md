The effects of anti-mining struggles over capital accumulation
and imperialism in the Andes
José Manuel Mejía V.
mejiavillena@ucsb.edu
Purpose of project/study
The projected study have two objectives: 1) the comparative analysis of three cases of anti-
mining contention, and 2) the Marxist analysis of the impact of these struggles over the rate
of profit of transnational mining corporations. The analysis of the class struggle involved in
anti-mining contentious campaigns from a historical political sociology, can orient
theoretically the evidence about the operation of the mechanism and processes of
contention between the indigeneous-peasantry and the imperialist mining bourgeoisie.
Nevertheless, the effects of the process of scale shift of anti-mining contention in the Andes
should be addressed by a Marxist analysis of the impact of these anti-mining campaigns of
struggle had over the profits of the transnational mining corporations since the beginnings
of the XXI century. Both necessities for the advancement in the study of extractive
contention in a multilevel polity approach are grounded also in the increasingly growing
literature on geo-economics and geopolitical current affairs which consider the centrality of
certain strategic resources with certain material features which determine its possibilities
for production and transportation.
Means of accomplishment
Three initial exploratory case studies are proposed considering field work and archive
research in Colombia, Ecuador and Peru. The first objective will be accomplished by a
combination of mixed methods starting with interviews to activists and scholars, I will
select a subnational circumscription to initiate a first archival research intended to build a
catalog of protest events useful to compare at least three campaigns of anti-mining
struggles. In this phase of my sociology master thesis, the second objective will be
accomplished by studying the contribution of Marx on the Book 3 of Capital and on his
Economic Manuscript of 1864-1865 on the configuration of the rate of profit and it's further
division, considering the contributions also made by the theories on imperialism, global
capitalism, transnational capitalist class and state, and of hegemonic transitions, but
focusing on the concrete analysis of transnational mining production of profits in the
Andes.

How my work will contribute to international understanding/and or
world peace
The main contribution of this study to the international understanding of the world current
affairs will be to develop a theoretically oriented and empirically prospective interpretation
of the antimining struggles as a sector of a broader field of extractive contention which is in
the core of the current geopolitical and geo-economic global affairs at a multilevel polity -
i.e.: the Ukrainian and Gaza-Palestina interstate conflicts-, and of the effects of this
contentious campaigns on the profits made by transnational mining companies, as a subset
of the extractive industry which is in voracious expansion since the 2000's.
How the grant will enhance successful completion of this project
