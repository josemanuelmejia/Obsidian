China’s Zijin Mining to expand lithium footprint by acquiring 24.82% of Zangge Mining

China’s Zijin Mining will acquire 24.82% of Chinese lithium miner and producer Zangge Mining for a total of 13.73 billion yuan ($1.88 billion), the former said on Friday January 17.

Zijin Mining already held an 0.18% stake in Zangge Mining. Upon completion of the acquisition, Zijin Mining will hold 25% of Zangge Mining.

Zangge has established battery-grade lithium carbonate capacity of 10,000 tonnes per year.

In addition, the company has plans under way for multiple projects. It plans to build 100,000 tpy designed capacity at Mamicuo Salt Lake in Tibet, for which it was in the process of applying for a mining permit, it said in its third-quarter 2024 report, released on October 26.

“The Mamicuo project has high lithium content and the advantage of low costs, and is expected to bring positive financial benefits,” Zijin Mining said.

In addition, Zangge has plans for a 60,000 tpy lithium salts project at Jiezechaka Salt Lake in Tibet, which will be a combination of 30,000 tpy of battery-grade lithium carbonate and 30,000 tpy of technical-grade lithium hydroxide.

It is also planning a 70,000 tpy lithium salts project at Longmucuo Salt Lake in Tibet.

“As a world-class mining company, Zijin’s acquisition can help speed up the development of Zangge’s existing projects, for example, its Mamicuo salt lake project,” Chandler Wu, Fastmarkets’ senior analyst for battery raw materials, said.

“Zangge Mining has extensive experience in salt lake development and low-cost lithium extraction technology from salt lakes with a lithium recovery rate of over 95%... Additionally, a lithium precipitation process has been developed by Zangge Mining, allowing for the direct production of battery-grade lithium carbonate from salt lakes,” Zijin Mining said.

Zangge noted that their development of existing projects will be complemented by Zijin Mining, which demonstrated a technological advantage over deep mining, mine beneficiation and metallurgy, and resource exploration and expansion.

Bet on EV metals

“Zijin’s acquisition of Zangge shows that Zijin is betting on the future of energy metals, especially on the lithium salt lake,” Fastmarkets’ Wu said.

Zijin Mining is targeting output of 280,000-300,000 tpy of lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE) by 2028, according to the company.

By acquiring Zangge Mining, Zijin Mining could become one of the most important lithium producers in the world, Zijin Mining said.

Zijin has completed construction of phase 1 of its Tres Quebradas lithium salt lake project in Catamarca, Argentina, with capacity of 20,000 tpy of battery-grade lithium carbonate.

Phase 2 of the project, which has a designed capacity of 30,000 tpy of battery-grade lithium carbonate, is under construction, according to Zijin Mining.

Its Lakkor Tso project has capacity of 20,000 tpy of battery-grade lithium hydroxide for phase 1.

Meanwhile, Zijin Mining plans to start production at its Manono project in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) during the first quarter of 2026, Bloomberg reported.

Fastmarkets’ analysts forecast a deficit of close to 40,000 tonnes of LCE in 2025, compared with a surplus of more than 100,000 tonnes of LCE in 2024. This is due to production cuts and improvements in demand from certain areas of the downstream supply chain starting to take effect.

Some market participants echoed the view that the lithium market could move into a more balanced supply/demand outlook in the short to medium term.

This swing in the overall market balance was also aided by relatively robust annual global growth in EV adoption and rapid annual growth in the energy storage system (ESS) sector.

But due to low lithium prices, the commissioning of the Tres Quebrads and the Lakkor Tso projects will be delayed until 2025, Zijin Mining said on October 18.

The company also said in August that it would lower its lithium output in the second half of 2024 under pressure from falling lithium prices.

Fastmarkets’ daily price assessment for lithium carbonate 99.5% Li₂CO₃ min, battery grade, spot price range exw domestic China was 77,000-79,000 yuan ($10,526-10,799) per tonne on Friday January 17, widening upward by 1,000 yuan per tonne from 77,000-78,000 yuan per tonne a day earlier.

The price has fallen by 32.17% from the 2024 peak at 111,000-119,000 yuan per tonne on March 14.
