   - Grassroots initiatives often struggle with inequitable resource allocation.
   - State and federal funding structures can impose restrictive conditions, limiting creativity and local autonomy.

### **Case Studies of Public Humanities in Action**
The essay highlights initiatives that illustrate the transformative potential of public humanities:
- **New Jersey's Reckoning with Slavery**:
   - A local project uncovered histories of free Black individuals being sold into slavery by a judge whose name adorned public spaces.
   - The community-led effort, supported by the New Jersey Council for the Humanities (NJCH), involved research, public education, and advocacy for renaming spaces and erecting memorials.
   - This initiative exemplifies co-creation of knowledge, where academic expertise supplemented community leadership.

- **Virginia's Educational Heritage Exhibit**:
   - Residents of Esmont, Virginia, commemorated a historically Black school slated for closure by creating a history exhibit honoring its namesake, Benjamin Franklin Yancey.
   - The project, funded by Virginia Humanities, utilized oral histories and archival research to preserve local Black history.

These examples showcase how public humanities can empower communities to reclaim narratives and address systemic inequities.
