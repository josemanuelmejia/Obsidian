Maintaining that division of labor internationally allowed also the replacement of the relations of production of feudalism in Western Europe by relations of capitalist production, mainly the development of waged labor, the fragmentation of land under the control of the feudal rural classes and the formation of a regime of private-small property and state building in the centers of mercantile accumulation for Western Europe; and the regime of hacienda, servitude, slavery and non-monetary forms of labor exploitation in the colonies at a world-scale.
