Why the obstructive performances of contention over critical commodities circulation have antisystemic effects on the reproduction of capital?

The obstruction of critical commodities' circulation and the political contention for its looting, are proper of wars. The uncertainty around the reality of trade impact directly on the transformation of surplus-value into profit. But, why obstructive performances have a causal effect on the fall of the rate of profit?
From a Marxist critique of the political economy of imperialism is critical, first, to understand that the reason why those interrumptions are cause of the fall in the rate of profit is because of the increased magnitude of the constant capital as a component of the total capital invested. And consequently on the magnitude of profit produced by a higher cost price given a determined total magnitude of capital advanced.
