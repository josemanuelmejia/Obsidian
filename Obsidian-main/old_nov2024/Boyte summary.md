Boyte stated that political conflict and social fragmentation trends toward polarization, can be reversed by taking the leadership to mobilize the tremendous opportunities, resources and responsibilities of colleges and universities toward the effort of building citizen professionalism, distinctive from the disciplinary professionalism approach nurtured by liberal education.

By a comparison between "heart" and "hand", as well as "head", Boyte attempted to give examples of their holistic integration in examples from Oklahoma and OSU.

Boyte's second objective pursued in this article was oriented to describe the technocratic feedback mechanisms of social fragmentation and polarization, that challenge the undertaking intellectual leadership in "bringing our nation back to the great wells of democracy". 

Full of patriotism, the democratic ideal superiority of the American Dream appears as its general wealth, relying on the incorporation capacity of its political regime. The asymmetries of power, inequalities, social fragmentation, political conflict, and polarization present concretely in social life, are mediated by the democratic regime, those forces and its trend looks very different under de-democratization processes. In which conditions, the approach of citizen professionalism would present higher challenges.

One of the most important constrains for the building of citizen professionalism is the structural limits imposed by power relations. As the author quoted:

"Virtually all began their careers wanting to have public impact. Almost all felt frustrated by the deepening culture of detachment whose norms hold “value neutrality” to be constitutive of scholarly excellence".
"Many, widely seen as leaders in their fields, told me that they would not be able even to discuss their discontents given the strong biases of research cultures of disciplines against extensive public engagement."

Because knowledge is not free from power. Will of truth is will of power. If knowledge is power then its use isn't unintended, neither built from scratch but from the force of violence for appropriation and reproduction. If the reproduction of certain human kind depends on the instrumentality of reason and knowledge for dominance, then the neutrality as an ideal found its limits as purposefully directed toward an uninterested will of truth, in the power relations as interested movements of knowledge toward dominance as dependent on the conservation of certain position in the knowledge hierarchies overlapping with technocracy, private bureaucracies, public governance, and also democratic governability.
